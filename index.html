This might sound familiar.

You're lying awake at 2am wondering if your kids can feel the tension. You're performing well at work while your home life quietly falls apart. Maybe you've tried therapy before. It helped for a minute. Then the same patterns came back.

You don't need someone to listen to you vent. You need someone who can see what you can't and help you change it.

You don't have a communication problem. You have a pattern problem.

Underneath the fights about dishes and bedtimes and money is a pattern — a stance and dance — that both of you have been running since long before you met each other. These patterns were smart once. But what protected you as a kid is now the thing driving your partner crazy.

You don't need your partner to become a different person. You need a shift of about 10% — and it can feel like everything changed.

This isn't open-ended talk therapy. There's a structure.

Grounded in Relational Life Therapy (RLT) — a direct, action-oriented approach developed by Terry Real.

R

Reveal

We map the deeper stances and cycles driving your conflicts. We name what's actually going on.

E

Explore

We work with the protective parts of you that don't want to change. This is where most coaching stops. It's where mine starts.

S

Strengthen

You build the ability to see yourself mid-reaction and choose something different. Real tools. Real practice between sessions.

E

Engage

You learn real repair. Actual accountability, actual vulnerability, actual movement back toward each other.

T

Tend

The relationship becomes a practice — not a problem to be fixed. You don't leave "cured." You leave equipped.

Things I don't do.

I don't sit quietly and let you vent for an hour. I'm direct — with kindness and respect.
I'm not a therapist. I don't diagnose conditions or prescribe medication.
I don't do this work for you. You get real homework. Change requires practice — not just insight.
I take sides. Not permanently, not unfairly — but I will name what's happening even when it's uncomfortable.
